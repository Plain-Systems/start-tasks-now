Why is starting so hard?

Starting feels hard when the first step is unclear or abstract. Your brain treats it as risk. This page makes the first step physical, small, and optional.

The real reason starting is hard

  • Abstract tasks create uncertainty
  • Uncertainty feels like risk
  • Risk triggers delay
  • Delay becomes procrastination

What to do right now (2 minutes)

  1. Name one physical action that proves you started.
  2. Make it under 2 minutes.
  3. Do it once, then stop if you want.

How to make any task startable

Do not “work on the task.” Convert it into a single trigger action.

Example: “work on project” becomes “open the document.”
