Can’t start tasks

If you keep thinking “I should do it” but you can’t begin, the problem is almost always the first step. This page gives you a mechanical way to start in under 2 minutes without motivation.

What this is

  • A mechanical way to start when you feel stuck
  • No motivation required
  • No planning, tracking, or willpower
  • Designed for people who freeze at the start

What to do right now (2 minutes)

  1. Choose one visible action you can do in under 2 minutes.
  2. Do it once.
  3. Stop immediately if you want. Starting is the win.

Why you can’t start

When a task is abstract, your brain treats it as risk. Risk creates delay.

The fastest fix is to shrink the task into one physical trigger action you can do without thinking.
